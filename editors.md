# For the Attention of Wiki Editors
Welcome! Your contributions are valued greatly, as you are helping improve the availability of documentation and tutorials for the wider community. Before you begin editing, you may have been told about the Discord server. If not, please join [here](https://discord.gg/XsZbvzetGX) as this is the central communication point for all content on the wiki. Additionally, the wiki has a few simple rules that keep things consistent and keeps everybody happy. These are listed below.

> If you need permission to do something that Wiki.js is not letting you do, ping me (UplandJacob) on Discord.
{.is-info}

## Client links/source code are not permitted

As mentioned on the introductory page for the wiki, client links and client source code are unavailable and not permitted to be added on the wiki. This is because of the location of where the wiki is hosted. Posting client links or source code would make the site vulnerable to DMCA. As a workaround, adding Discord invite links to client Discords is allowed. This will eventually change, as we plan to move behind bulletproof hosting, and as such, the rules will be changed to reflect this.

## Keep content appropriate

We want to keep the site clean and free of any profanity or inappropriate content. This means even the most basic of curse words. This ensures that we are keeping the documentation open to the maximum amount of people.

## Be respectful of other contributions

If you feel like information provided within a contribution may be incorrect, you are more than welcome to discuss with the person who made the contribution/edit on the page. However, simply overwriting the contribution with no warning is disrespectful, and thus may result in disputes/further consequences. As a result, we ask that any disagreements over contributions are handled and resolved before any further changes are made to pages.

## Remain unbiased

You must not show bias towards a particular client, service, website or user. This keeps the wiki informative and allows for greater adoption of its resources. By having a bias within the wiki, it can limit the progression of the community and make users feel like their choices are limited based on what is dictated by the wiki.

## Be consistent

Most of the webpages use the Markdown editor. We strongly advise, that for the consistency of the website, that we all follow the same rule of thumb, and stick to Markdown. This means that changes can be made and nobody has to learn anything new in order to make changes to a previous contribution.

> Please note, this is not an extensive list of rules, and more rules will be added as required.
> {.is-warning}
